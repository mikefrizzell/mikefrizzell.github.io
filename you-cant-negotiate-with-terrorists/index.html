You can't negotiate with terrorists

February 3, 2023 Michael Frizzell
Politics Society

Every time a video emerges of police brutalizing and killing someone, liberals are quick to jump in and condemn the actions while defending the police state. “This is bad, and we need to fix policing in America”, they say from their suburban neighborhoods that only see cops when someone spots a poor person walking on the wrong street.

“We need more police”, they say without any hint of sarcasm. Yes, we need more armed psychopaths with licenses to kill. We need unaccountable men to go around and harass homeless people and make sure businesses are well protected against black teenagers.

“But if we reform policing in America, things will get better.” No, it won’t. It just won’t. You can’t reform something that is, by its very nature, irredeemable. The police don’t want reform and they’ll do everything they can to stop it. Look at what happened after the George Floyd protests, cops just stopped doing their jobs and held cities hostage.

You can’t negotiate with these terrorists because everything they have is predicated on them having absolute power. They aren’t going to willingly lay down their arms and try something different. These people are police because they can brutalize people, not in spite of it. Or, to use a phrase that’s kind of over played: the cruelty is the point.

The only way to move forward is to tear the system down and build something else. Patching things up won’t work.
