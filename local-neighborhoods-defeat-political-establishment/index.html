Local neighborhoods defeat political establishment

August 5, 2022 Michael Frizzell
Ozarks Politics Society

In a crushing victory reminiscent of the rebels destroying the first Death Star, neighborhood activists in central Springfield have defeated a plan to open up a 7 Brew drive through coffee place on Sunshine & Jefferson in Springfield. I’m not being sarcastic, this is really good news and shows that the people do have some power over the builders and developers who run this city. A rare win, and one that should give us courage in the future.

Royce Redding, local weasel and former Billy Long staffer, owns the property that was to be turned into a drive through. The lot, which has been vacant for years and occasionally serves as a place for community yard sales, is zoned as residential and Redding wanted the zoning changed to allow a new 7 Brew. (Because there aren’t enough already.)

The plan for the lot, after multiple rejections by the zoning board, was to have an entrance and exit on both Jefferson and Roanoke. Roanoke, a small residential street. This was done presumably because anyone of average intelligence could see that Sunshine wouldn’t be able to handle the added traffic. So they tried to pawn it off onto neighborhood streets. They also magnanimously offered to plant some trees on the back of the property.

Of course, none of this would benefit the people who live in the surrounding area, myself included. Their own research shows the majority of customers would be people driving along Sunshine. I wouldn’t be able to hop on my bike and ride over for a coffee because it’s a drive through only. I understand that no one is going to build a home on that corner (although an apartment building?), but a business should benefit the neighborhood.

I’m not glad we won because I’m some sort of home-value NIMBY. I was OK with the apartments built along the Greenway off of Sunset. I was OK with the 55+ duplexes on the other side of the street. I wouldn’t do anything to fight against more housing or responsible businesses because that’s good for the community. What’s not good for the community is another drive through restaurant with a line of cars flowing out into the streets. Starbucks, McDonalds, and the chicken nugget places already do enough of that.

It also doesn’t hurt that Royce Redding lost.
